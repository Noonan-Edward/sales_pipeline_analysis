# Insights, Recommendations, & Concluding Remarks

### Sales Pipeline Analysis Key Insights

#### Overview

Over 8800 opportunities in the sales pipeline:
- 500 are merely prospecting
- 1589 are currently engaging
- 6711 are closed

Out of those 6711 closed opportunities:
- 4238 have won (63%)
- 2473 have lost (37%)

This data spans 437 days. In other words, roughly 1y 2mo.

During this time, the pipeline generated roughly ~$10M in sales revenue.

That's close to $8M/y.

Most deals are engaged for 45-55 days, but certain factors (product, account, etc.) can significantly affect this duration.

#### Pipeline Health & Customer Segmentation

Accounts like Hottechi, Kan-Code, Konex, and Condax close most frequently. However, out of those 4, Konex closes the quickest. Of those 4, Kan-Code closes the largest deals. These are reliable, repeat customers.

The big three industries are retail, technology, and medical. Medical, though, suffers a lower win rate than retail and technology.

Though deals are won more often with enterprise-sized (large) firms, bigger firms don't necessarily close on large deals, on average.

#### Products

The GTK 500 is a ticket to a high-value sale, but it never sells; thus, it generates no revenue. Moreover, the GTK 500 makes for a long sales process.

On the other hand, the GTX Pro creates a substantial close value while closing quickly, and sells out impressively often. The GTX Pro is, without a doubt, the breadwinner.

However, the GTX Basic also makes its presence known with a ton of sales. The only downside to the GTX Basic is that it's a significantly lower-priced model. Thus, it doesn't create the revenue upside the GTX Pro does.

Generally speaking, higher-priced products don't necessarily win at a lower rate than others. The outstanding exception is the GTK 500. It oftentimes presents itself as an effective waste of time for the salesperson, the team, and the client.

#### Teams/Regions and Sales Reps

West, Central, and East regions frankly don't vary dramatically in revenue yield.

However, the West appears more efficient, with "per-day-engaged" values sitting non-negligibly higher than those of the East and Central regions.

The West's time is more valuable than that of the other regions.

The Central region, however, has closed the most deals by far. Logically following is that the West is where the bigger deals are going down.

Individually, sales rep. Darcel Schlecht has closed on the most (553) opportunities by far, but with a merely average win-rate. They've generated the most value for the company over the last 437 days ($1M+, over 2x their runner-up), emphasizing quantity over quality.

Lajuna Vencill, Markita Hansen, Donn Cantrell, Gladys Colclough, and Niesha Huffines have closed on many opportunities, but with some of the lowest win rates in the bunch (<60%).

Violet McLelland, Wilburn Farren, Niesha Huffines, Versie Hillebrand, Lajuana Vencill, and Garret Kinder have produced the least value (<$166K/y).

McLelland, in particular, has hardly reached $100K/y.

### Concluding Remarks

Generally speaking, for a B2B sales pipeline, a 63% win rate is rare and remarkable. Focusing on the Retail, Tech, and Medical industries seems to be the most reliable course of action, especially if sales agent delegation and management stay under wraps. Letting the highest win-rate closers jump on the lowest win-rate sectors (medical) could prove to be a leap into revenue improvement, whereas keeping the highest volume closers on the other big sectors (retail and tech) is the safest bet for maintaining revenue elsewhere. One product to keep an eye on is the GTK 500. Unless it's produced specifically to order, it might be creeping towards the brink of extinction. It appears the consensus on the product highlights its undesirability and potential over-pricedness. Otherwise, maintenance and baby-step improvements to the sales teams' performances should keep the business trajectory healthy.
